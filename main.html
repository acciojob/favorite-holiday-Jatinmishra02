Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto, once the capital of Japan, is a city on the island of Honshu. It's famous for its numerous classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses. It's also known for its formal traditions such as kaiseki dining, consisting of multiple courses of precise dishes, and geisha female entertainers often found in the Gion district.

  1. Fushimi Inari Shrine - Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates.
  2. Kinkaku-ji - The Golden Pavilion, a Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf.
  3. Kiyomizu-dera - An iconic temple with a wooden stage that juts out from its main hall.
  4. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove - A serene and breathtaking bamboo forest.
  5. Nijo Castle - A historical castle known for its beautiful gardens and architecture.
  • Kaiseki - Traditional multi-course Japanese dinner.
  • Yudofu - A hot tofu dish that is a Kyoto specialty.
  • Shojin Ryori - Buddhist vegetarian cuisine.
  • Matcha - Powdered green tea used in tea ceremonies.
  • Yatsuhashi - A type of Japanese confectionery made from glutinous rice flour.
